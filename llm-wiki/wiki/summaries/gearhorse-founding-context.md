## What this source covers

The origin, brand identity, product line, business plan, exit strategy, and subscriber retention strategy for [[Gear Horse]] — a pre-launch American-made camping gear company.

---

## Key takeaways

### Origin
The company was conceived at a campfire on the Deschutes National Forest by [[Eric Thompson]] and co-founders. Three product ideas crystallized in one evening and were saved to a phone Notes app at 6% battery. The name came from a stranger at a trailhead with a horse carrying implausible amounts of gear.

**Founding insight:** The camping gear market is dominated by Asian manufacturing, leaving a gap for premium, US-made specialty accessories that solve real campfire problems.

### Products
Three products with distinct manufacturing paths and margin targets:

- [[Trail Maul]] — mini splitting maul, $89–$109, 55–65% GM, drop-forge US steel
- [[Ember Lid]] — folding cast-iron campfire pizza lid, $74–$94, 50–60% GM, US foundry
- [[Chair Armor]] — model-specific camp chair covers, $38–$54, 60–70% GM, cut-and-sew USA

### Business model
[[Zero-Capital Launch Strategy]] across 4 phases: validate with email list → Kickstarter crowdfund → first manufacturing run + Shopify → organic growth. Zero manufacturing spend until 500+ email subscribers validate demand.

### Exit strategy
Target acquisition by an outdoor brand within 5 years at $3M–$5M ARR with 60%+ gross margins and clean IP. Likely acquirers: Lodge, Benchmade, Duluth Trading, Stanley, Solo Stove, BioLite, Snow Peak.

### Retention research
[[Community Over Subscription]]: subscription boxes drive churn; community and brand narrative drive loyalty. Key pattern: segment subscribers at signup by activity type, lead with story, use ambassadors (not influencers), and sell a consumables subscription tied to product ownership.

### Tech stack
[[Carrd Make Notion Stack]]: Carrd Pro Lite (landing pages) + Make (automation) + Notion (CRM) + Brevo (email) + GA4 + Clarity (analytics) + Stripe (payments).
