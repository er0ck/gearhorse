# Made in USA Brand Positioning

The core competitive moat for [[Gear Horse]]. The camping gear market is dominated by Asian manufacturing. US-made specialty accessories command a premium, have a defensible narrative, and are harder to copy in ways that damage IP value for an acquirer.

---

## The founding insight

The gap in the market is not product category (mauls, lids, and chair covers all exist) but origin and quality tier. No one is making these products in the USA at craft scale for the premium camping audience.

---

## Why it matters for exit value

Chinese knockoffs post-acquisition are a primary acquirer fear. A clean IP portfolio (provisionals in Year 1, utility patents in Year 2) removes that fear. [[Made in USA Brand Positioning]] + IP + defensible margins is the combination that produces a "real offer" at $3M–$5M ARR.

---

## FTC compliance

Apply for the FTC "Made in USA" seal in Phase 4 of the [[Zero-Capital Launch Strategy]], once reviews and sales history are established.

---

## Brand voice tie-in

The brand voice is built around the manufacturing origin: "we want to find that guy [the American forge worker]. We want to pay him." The sourcing story is content. It's uncopiable and is the Year 1 content calendar.

See: [[Community Over Subscription]] for how the origin story functions as a retention tool.
