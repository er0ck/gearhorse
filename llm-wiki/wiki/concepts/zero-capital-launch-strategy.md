# Zero-Capital Launch Strategy

A 4-phase launch plan for [[Gear Horse]] designed to reach initial revenue without upfront manufacturing investment. The core principle: validate demand before spending money on inventory.

---

## Phase 1 — Validate (Months 1–2, $0)

- Build landing page (Carrd Pro Lite, $19/year)
- Post organic, problem-focused content on r/camping and r/bushcraft — no product pitch, no link, just the problem
- Goal: 500+ email subscribers before any manufacturing spend

**Why 500 first:** Manufacturing a first run without an audience is the most common way new physical product brands lose money. The email list is proof of demand.

---

## Phase 2 — Crowdfund (Months 2–4)

- Kickstarter campaign for whichever product ([[Trail Maul]] or [[Ember Lid]]) validated strongest in Phase 1
- Funding goal: $8,000–$15,000
- Early-bird pricing: 15–20% below retail
- Kickstarter fees (~8–10%) charged only on success — zero risk on failure

---

## Phase 3 — First Run & Store (Months 4–6)

- Shopify Basic ($39/month)
- 50–200 unit first production run
- Net-30 payment terms with manufacturers where possible
- Stripe / Shop Pay for payments

---

## Phase 4 — Organic Growth (Months 6–12)

- 10 free units to mid-tier creators (10k–100k followers) in camping/bushcraft niches
- Klaviyo email (free tier to 500 contacts)
- Etsy as a secondary channel
- Paid Meta/Google ads once 20+ reviews are collected
- Apply for FTC "Made in USA" seal

---

## Why this works

- Eliminates inventory risk in Phase 1 and 2
- Kickstarter provides both capital and social proof
- Organic Reddit strategy builds an audience without ad spend
- Each phase gates the next, so costs only grow with validated revenue

---

## Connection to exit strategy

This strategy is optimized for a 5-year acquisition exit. The early discipline around [[Made in USA Brand Positioning]], IP filing, and clean books (S-Corp or C-Corp from day one) all serve that goal. See [[Gear Horse]] for the full exit framework.
