# Community Over Subscription

The core retention insight for [[Gear Horse]]: subscription boxes alone don't build loyalty, community does. The subscription and email list are infrastructure. The brand narrative is the product.

---

## What works for outdoor brands

### Segment at signup
A simple preference question at opt-in ("car camper, backpacker, or homesteader?") enables segmentation. Relevant emails dramatically outperform generic blasts. Automated segmented messages drive a disproportionate share of revenue relative to their volume.

### Lead with story, not product
Arc'teryx, YETI, The North Face all lead with adventure narratives. For [[Gear Horse]]: document the founding story, manufacturer sourcing trips, first campfire tests. This content is uncopiable and serves as the Year 1 content calendar.

### Ambassadors over influencers
Long-term relationships with 5–8 real campers/bushcrafters (5k–50k followers). A series beats one-off posts. Let the ambassador live a whole arc, not just a highlight reel.

### UGC as trust engine
Branded hashtag (e.g. #MadeToSplit, #EmberLidCook). Feature customer adventures back to them in email. Social proof from peers converts better than brand claims.

### Email sequences that teach, not sell
Example for [[Ember Lid]] buyers: 4-email sequence covering fire-building basics → campfire pizza recipe → cast iron care → gentle [[Trail Maul]] cross-sell. First three emails sell nothing.

---

## The right Gear Horse subscription model

NOT a generic gear box. Generic boxes drive churn because subscribers receive irrelevant products ("too many knives" is a documented churn driver from Nomadik research).

Instead: **consumables subscription tied to product ownership.** Quarterly seasoning oil + fire-starter kit for Ember Lid owners. Defensible because it requires owning the gear first — a subscriber who doesn't own the product won't subscribe.

---

## Retention one-liner

> Sign people up with an honest founding story, segment them immediately by activity, send genuinely useful content between purchases, feature their real adventures back to them, and sell a consumables subscription that only makes sense if they already own your gear.
