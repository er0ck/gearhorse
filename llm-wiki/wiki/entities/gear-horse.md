# Gear Horse

American-made camping gear brand, pre-launch as of 2026-04-14. Founded in Oregon. DTC e-commerce + Kickstarter crowdfunding model.

The `.camp` TLD was chosen as on-brand and memorable. The name comes from a story about a stranger at a trailhead with a horse carrying an implausible amount of camping gear.

---

## Products

- [[Trail Maul]] — mini splitting maul (~2.5 lbs, drop-forged US steel), $89–$109, 55–65% GM
- [[Ember Lid]] — folding cast-iron campfire pizza lid (~12"×12"), $74–$94, 50–60% GM
- [[Chair Armor]] — model-specific camp chair covers (waxed canvas or Cordura), $38–$54, 60–70% GM

The founding insight: the camping gear market is dominated by Asian manufacturing, leaving a gap for premium US-made accessories that solve real campfire problems.

---

## Founding

Conceived at a campfire on the Deschutes National Forest by [[Eric Thompson]] and co-founders. Three product ideas emerged in a single evening, saved to Notes at 6% battery. Name came from a group brainstorm; "Chair Armor" got unanimous finger guns, the highest honor.

## Business Strategy

### Launch phases
See [[Zero-Capital Launch Strategy]] for the full 4-phase plan:
1. Validate with landing page + organic Reddit posts (goal: 500 email subscribers)
2. Kickstarter crowdfund ($8K–$15K target, Trail Maul or Ember Lid)
3. First manufacturing run + Shopify store
4. Organic growth via creators, email, Etsy, then paid ads

### Exit strategy
Acquisition by outdoor brand within 5 years. Target: $3M–$5M ARR, 60%+ gross margins, clean IP.

Likely acquirers: Lodge, Benchmade, Duluth Trading, Stanley, Solo Stove, BioLite, Snow Peak.

Valuation framework: 2–5x revenue is a common pattern for brands with strong identity and defensible margins.
