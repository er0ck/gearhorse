# Trail Maul

Mini splitting maul by [[Gear Horse]]. The first product targeted for Kickstarter launch.

---

## Problem it solves

No camp-scale splitting maul exists at retail. Hatchets can't split rounds. Full-size mauls are impractical to transport to a campsite. Campers who want split firewood have no good tool option.

---

## Specs

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Weight | ~2.5 lbs |
| Material | Drop-forged American steel |
| Handle | Hickory or fiberglass |
| Retail price target | $89–$109 |
| Gross margin target | 55–65% |

---

## Manufacturing path

ThomasNet → drop-forge shops in PA, OH, TN. These are ex-Craftsman/Sears suppliers with excess capacity.

---

## IP

File provisional patent (~$320 via USPTO) in Year 1. Convert to utility patent (~$5,000) by Year 2.

## Name origin

"It rhymes and it works." Conceived at the founding campfire.

## Launch sequencing

The Trail Maul or [[Ember Lid]] will be the Kickstarter lead product, whichever validates more strongly via email list engagement.
